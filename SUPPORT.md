# Support

waitbus is maintained by a single developer as time allows. This document
points you at the right channel and sets clear expectations.

## Channels

| For | Channel |
|-----|---------|
| **Security vulnerabilities** | Do **not** open a public issue. Use GitHub's private vulnerability reporting and follow the coordinated-disclosure process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). |
| **Abuse or conduct concerns** | Email `sankalp.gilda@gmail.com` with subject `[waitbus conduct]`. See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) for what is in scope and how reports are handled. |
| **General questions, bugs, feature requests** | Open a GitHub issue on the repository. Search existing issues first. |

The three channels currently route to the same maintainer; they are split
by label and intent so the right context reaches the right place. A
project-domain email alias (e.g. `security@` / `abuse@`) is **not yet
provisioned** — it depends on the final project name and a registered
domain (tracked alongside the naming decision), so until then use the
addresses above. When the alias lands it slots in here without changing
the channel split.

## Support model

- **This is a pre-1.0, single-maintainer project.** Support is best-effort,
  not contractual: there is no SLA and no paid tier.
- **Security reports** get an acknowledgement within 72 hours and follow
  the disclosure timeline in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
- **Bugs** are triaged as time allows; a clear, minimal reproduction gets a
  faster response. An issue without enough detail to reproduce may be closed
  with a request for more information.
- **Feature requests** are welcome but not guaranteed. The
  [roadmap](ROADMAP.md) reflects current priorities and direction.
